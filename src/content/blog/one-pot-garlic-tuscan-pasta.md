# One-Pot Garlic Tuscan Pasta

**Prep:** 5 min | **Cook:** 20 min | **Total:** 25 min | **Serves:** 4

Everything cooks in one pot. You don't even drain the pasta.

---

## Ingredients

- 1 lb penne or rigatoni
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
- 2 cups chicken broth (or vegetable broth)
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 3 cups fresh spinach (or 1 cup frozen)
- 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes, chopped
- 1/2 cup grated parmesan
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Equipment

- [Large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B000N501BK?tag=cookfromwhat2-20) (5-6 quart minimum)
- Wooden spoon
- [Microplane or parmesan grater](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00004S7V8?tag=cookfromwhat2-20)

---

## Instructions

**1. Build the base (3 min).** Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add garlic and cook 60 seconds until fragrant. Don't let it brown.

**2. Add liquids and pasta (2 min).** Pour in the chicken broth, diced tomatoes with their juice, and Italian seasoning. Bring to a boil. Add the pasta and stir so nothing sticks to the bottom.

**3. Cook the pasta (12-14 min).** Reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer, stirring every 3-4 minutes to prevent sticking. The pasta absorbs most of the liquid as it cooks. This is the whole point of one-pot: the starch thickens the sauce for you.

**4. Finish (3 min).** When the pasta is al dente and most liquid is absorbed, stir in the heavy cream, sun-dried tomatoes, and spinach. The spinach wilts in about 90 seconds. Add the parmesan and red pepper flakes. Stir until creamy.

**5. Serve.** Taste for salt. Top with extra parmesan and a drizzle of olive oil.

---

## Technique Tip: Why You Don't Drain One-Pot Pasta

The starchy pasta water is the sauce. When pasta cooks directly in a measured amount of liquid, the starch released from the noodles thickens everything into a creamy coating. This is the same principle behind cacio e pepe and risotto. If you add too much liquid, you lose this effect and end up with soup. If you add too little, the pasta sticks and burns. The ratio here (2 cups broth + 14 oz canned tomatoes for 1 lb pasta) is calibrated so the liquid is almost fully absorbed right when the pasta hits al dente. Stir every few minutes to distribute the starch evenly.

---

## Substitutions

- **No heavy cream?** Use half-and-half or a splash of pasta water with extra parmesan for thickness.
- **No sun-dried tomatoes?** Skip them. The canned diced tomatoes carry the tomato flavor.
- **No fresh spinach?** Frozen spinach works. Squeeze out excess water first.
- **Make it protein-heavy:** Add shredded rotisserie chicken in step 4 or cook Italian sausage in step 1 before the garlic.

---

## Storage

Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Reheat on the stovetop with a splash of broth or water to loosen the sauce. The pasta absorbs liquid as it sits.
